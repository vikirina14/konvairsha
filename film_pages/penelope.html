PENELOPE

Penelope is a 2006 fantasy romantic comedy directed by Mark Palansky and starring Christina Ricci in the title role. The film tells the story of a young woman born under a curse that can only be broken if she finds true love ‚Äî but her unusual appearance makes this task nearly impossible.

Penelope is a charming mix of fairy tale whimsy and modern storytelling, exploring themes of self-acceptance, beauty, and the meaning of true love.

Set in an alternate version of Victorian London, Penelope Wilhern is born into a wealthy family cursed for generations. As punishment for their arrogance, each female child is born with the face of a pig. The family keeps Penelope hidden from the world, hoping to break the curse by finding her a suitor willing to marry her despite her appearance.

After years of failed attempts to find a husband, Penelope decides to leave the safety of her home and explore the world on her own. Along the way, she meets a variety of quirky characters and learns to accept herself ‚Äî ultimately realizing that happiness doesn't depend on others' approval.

The film features a stellar supporting cast including James McAvoy, Catherine O'Hara, and Reese Witherspoon (as executive producer), and blends dark humor with heartfelt moments. Its unique visual style and imaginative set design give it a fairytale-like quality.

Though not a box office hit, *Penelope* has gained a cult following over time, praised for its originality, emotional depth, and bold approach to themes of identity and acceptance. It remains a standout example of British indie filmmaking with a touch of magic realism.

Interesting Facts

  • Christina Ricci wore prosthetic makeup to play Penelope, which took over three hours to apply each day.
  • The film was shot entirely in the UK, with many scenes filmed at Shepperton Studios and other iconic British locations.
  • Reese Witherspoon was so impressed by the script that she signed on as an executive producer and helped secure distribution for the film.
  • The set design was inspired by classic fairy tales and steampunk aesthetics, creating a unique visual world.
  • Despite mixed reviews upon release, *Penelope* has since become a favorite among fans of fantasy romance and body-positive cinema.
